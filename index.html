Our Vision

A New Kenya for All

KCW envisions a transformed nation — just, prosperous, and democratically transparent. Three pillars anchor everything we do.

01 ⚖️

Social Justice

A New Kenya where justice, rule of law, and constitutional freedoms are guaranteed to every citizen — from the least to the greatest — through equal application of the law, building a cohesive, well-knit society.

02 📈

Economic Justice

A New Kenya where all who wish to work find meaningful employment, and local investors can thrive in a prosperous business environment — driving household and national economic growth for all.

03 🗳️

Electoral Justice

A New Kenya with fair, credible, transparent elections — where national poll tallying streams live on every registered voter's mobile device, eliminating disputed results and restoring democratic trust.

Mabonga C Mabonga Founder & National Coordinator
About KCW

WHO WE ARE
& WHY WE EXIST

KCW is a peoples' driven sociopolitical movement seeking to catalyze citizen participation on matters of social justice, good governance, and faith through advocacy of ethical governance.

We work through Public Policy Debates, Political Consultancy Services, our DCCA Project, and annual IFK/B4 County and National Conferences — reaching every corner of Kenya's 47 counties.

Kenya's time for change is not tomorrow — it is now. Every day we wait is a day someone is denied justice, prosperity, or a fair vote.

— Mabonga C Mabonga, Founder

What We Do

OUR 4 PROGRAMMES

View All Programmes
01

Public Policy Debates (PPD)

Hosting independent public policy debates that build civic education and capacity on emerging political discourse across Kenya.

02

Grassroot Political Consultancy

Providing unmatched political consultancy services to new and established politicians — from election campaigns to crisis management.

03

IFK/B4 Annual Conferences

49 annual conferences countrywide: 47 county-level and 2 national — advocating patriotic citizen participation and nationbuilding.

04

DCCA Project M&E

Pushing for constitutional reforms through the DCCA initiative to provide better separation of powers and economic opportunity for all Kenyans.
